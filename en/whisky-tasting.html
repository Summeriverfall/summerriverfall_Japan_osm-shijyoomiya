Vintage Japanese Whisky Tasting

A Japanese Whisky Journey Through Time

Rare old bottles, guided comparison, and a time-structured tasting of Japanese whisky — from heritage, to terroir, to the future.

Discover a quieter, rarer side of Japanese whisky in Kyoto Shijo-Omiya.

This is more than a whisky flight. It is a guided journey through the past, present, and future of Japanese whisky: from the accumulated heritage of Hibiki, Yamazaki, and Nikka; to the present transformation of coastal terroir in Akkeshi; and toward the living growth of Chichibu and a new generation of Japanese craft distilleries.

Each tasting is arranged not only by rarity, but by sequence, contrast, timing, and story.

The major houses of Suntory and Nikka reveal how Japanese whisky inherited, refined, and transformed tradition over generations. Newer distilleries such as Akkeshi and Chichibu show how Japanese whisky is still changing — shaped by climate, place, craft, and future possibility.

This is not an ordinary whisky flight.

Why come here to taste Japanese whisky?

Because our stock runs three times deeper than a typical bar

Our Hibiki holdings run roughly three times deeper than a standard whisky specialty bar — from vintage Hibiki, Gold Label, and Gold Foil to early 17-Year releases, with multiple rare versions available to open and pour on any given visit.

Why taste Japanese whisky in Kyoto?

Because Hibiki and Yamazaki were born right here in Kansai

The Yamazaki Distillery is less than an hour away. Osaka has been Suntory's most important market since the brand's founding. Drinking Suntory here isn't just tasting whisky — it's experiencing a century of craft in the place where it all began.

Who leads the tasting experience?

More Than a Pour — A Conversation

With a background in investment banking and private equity, your host approaches whisky with a sharp eye for rarity, structure, and value. He has also worked across tea, dining, and hospitality, and brings together interests in marathon running, collecting, music, tea culture, antiques, vintage bottles, and Showa-era aesthetics. With a distinctly cross-cultural outlook and ease across multiple languages and dialects, he shapes each tasting with both depth and warmth.

Tasting Flights

Heritage at the Heart of Every Flight

Heritage is central to every flight we design — not just age or rarity. Every flight also holds a surprise bonus pour waiting to be discovered.

About our design philosophy

For us, heritage means more than age or rarity. It means understanding where Japanese whisky came from, how it developed, how it flourished, how it declined, and how it was revived. It means tracing both the horizontal stories across distilleries and styles, and the vertical stories that unfold across time.

This is why our flights are built not only around bottles, but around origins, lineages, transitions, and continuity.

THEME 04

Origin & Legacy

Lifelong Original Spirit — Taketsuru

Taketsuru Pure Malt is one of the most founder-symbolic brands within Nikka, named after Masataka Taketsuru, the “father of Japanese whisky.” It is blended from malt whiskies from Yoichi and Miyagikyo, combining Yoichi’s weight and smoke with Miyagikyo’s fruit and softness. By comparison, Super Nikka is a more classic premium blended whisky, round, gentle, and suitable for daily drinking. The Blend of Nikka Maltbase is an old-school malt-driven blend, showing more malt, wood, and smoke. G&G and Black Nikka are closer to popular blended whiskies, positioned more for highballs and everyday consumption. The core value of Taketsuru is not simple drinkability, but its role as a pure malt expression that condenses the styles of Nikka’s two distilleries and Masataka Taketsuru’s blending philosophy.

approx. 45–75 min

Show bottle details Click to collapse

Regular

Taketsuru White Label

Released on March 31, 2020 as the current revised Taketsuru Pure Malt, and still in circulation today. Compared with the Black Label, it is more modern and clearer in style, with apple, apricot, vanilla, banana, orange peel, malty body, and soft peat. The finish brings a dark-chocolate-like bitterness and woody tone.

Taketsuru Black Label

Taketsuru Black Label was the previous NAS Taketsuru before the current White Label, and was replaced in March 2020. Its discontinuation was mainly due to malt stock shortages and product restructuring: Nikka needed a more flexible NAS recipe to maintain the Taketsuru brand, instead of continuing to consume limited aged malt stocks. Compared with the White Label, the Black Label is more old-school and rounder, combining fruit, malty sweetness, vanilla oak, and gentle smoke. The texture is smooth, without the stronger Yoichi-driven weight and peated finish of the White Label. It works well as a comparison point for the previous generation of Taketsuru style.

Taketsuru 12

Taketsuru 12 Year Old was released in 2000 as the first age-statement expression in the Taketsuru series, and was discontinued around 2014 before being replaced by NAS Taketsuru. The main reason was a shortage of malt whiskies aged 12 years or more, requiring Nikka to reduce consumption of age-statement stocks. Its style is light, elegant, and easy-drinking, with bright fruit and soft malty sweetness. The emphasis is balance and smoothness rather than deep, heavy maturation.

Taketsuru 17

Taketsuru 17 Year Old was discontinued at the end of March 2020 and has not returned as a regular release. The reason was the rapid depletion of mature stock, making it difficult to maintain a stable 17-year-old standard. Compared with the 12-year-old, it has more depth, combining mature fruit, honey, vanilla, aged wood, gentle peat, and soft smoke. It is one of the best expressions of balance within the Taketsuru range.

Taketsuru 21

Taketsuru 21 Year Old was also discontinued at the end of March 2020 and has not returned as a regular release. The reason is the same as with the 17-year-old, but more severe: Yoichi and Miyagikyo malt whiskies aged 21 years or more are even rarer and could no longer support regular supply. Its style is deeper, softer, and more complex, with ripe fruit, dried fruit, honey, wood, dark chocolate, and light smoke. The long finish emphasizes the depth and harmony of high maturation.

Premium

Miyagikyo 10th Anniversary vintage Bottle

Miyagikyo Distillery was completed in 1969 as Nikka’s second distillery, built by Masataka Taketsuru in his later years to create malt whisky that was softer, fruitier, and more suitable for blending than Yoichi. In 1979, Miyagikyo reached its 10th anniversary, which was also the year Masataka Taketsuru passed away. This vintage bottle is therefore not only a Miyagikyo anniversary whisky, but also a historical snapshot of Taketsuru’s late-life ideal. It concentrates the Miyagikyo side of delicate fruit, soft body, and old-school Nikka elegance, making the blending philosophy behind Taketsuru Pure Malt more concrete and tangible.

THEME 07

Regional Craft

Mountain, Sea & Fireworks — Saburomaru SAB.

SAB. is Saburomaru Distillery’s smoky blended whisky series, inspired by the nature, seasons, and craft culture of the Tonami Plain in Toyama. Compared with the single malt and collectible character of the Tarot Series, SAB. is a more approachable daily gateway into the Saburomaru style. The series includes five distinct blended whiskies: SNOW WHITE, SUNSET RED, NIGHT BLACK, DARK BLACK, and OCEAN BLUE. This flight shows how Saburomaru turns its core smoky style into blended whiskies that are easier to drink and also well suited to highballs.

Hyakuseifuma “LESS IS MORE”

Hyakuseifuma is a limited series released by RUDDER to commemorate roughly 100 years of Japanese whisky history. The name suggests value that remains unpolished and undiminished even after a hundred generations. This “LESS IS MORE” is the 10th release in the series, produced and blended by Saburomaru Distillery. It uses 2018 Saburomaru malt as the key malt, from the final year of the old modified stills, blended with Scotch peated malt aged over 5 years and sherry-cask malt aged over 7 years, then further matured for 3 years at Saburomaru Distillery. Compared with the softer and more approachable SAB. series, it is woodier, peatier, more mature, and more fruit-driven, with notes of shaved wood, pine needles, burning wood, cooked apple, apricot candy, ripe banana, seaweed, dry grass, tobacco, tar, and coffee-like aftertaste. It is an ideal upgrade glass for moving from SAB. into Saburomaru’s higher-level blended malt world.

THEME 08

ZEMON Craft

Impermanence with a Path — Saburomaru Tarot

The Saburomaru Tarot Series is the distillery’s flagship single malt line, with each release named and labelled after the Major Arcana of the tarot. From THE FOOL and THE MAGICIAN to THE STRENGTH, each edition reflects a different distillation year and the technical experiments of the distillery at that time. The standard editions show balance, structure, and maturation more clearly, while the CS editions retain the more direct power of cask-strength whisky. This flight is designed to trace the recent evolution of Saburomaru: smoke, wood, fruit, body, and the experimental character of a new-generation Japanese whisky distillery in Toyama.

approx. 45–75 min

Show bottle details Click to collapse

Regular

Tarot VIII THE STRENGTH

Made with Islay 80 ppm Super Heavily Peated malt, this is the most maritime and intensely smoky release in the Tarot Series. The standard 48% edition is smoother, with notes of vanilla, bekko-ame hard candy, and grapefruit pith.

Tarot VII THE CHARIOT

Made with Highland 80 ppm Super Heavily Peated malt, this is one of the most powerful peated expressions in Saburomaru’s history. The nose shows burnt wood, ash, fern-like peat, orange peel, ripe red apple, and ginger. The palate is round, yet dense ashy peat spreads across the mouth, with a strong smoky finish that lingers over the tongue.

Tarot VI THE LOVERS

Distilled in 2020/2021, this edition combines land-driven Highland peat with maritime Islay peat. Its official theme is “souls that attract and resonate with each other,” making it an experiment in blending two different peat characters. The nose offers turmeric, refined smoke, rich vanilla, faint curry spice, beef jerky, wafers, kanro candy, ripe pineapple, and a subtle charred note. The palate shows elegant smoke, honey, wafers, beef jerky, and ripe pineapple. It is a more complex, umami-driven Saburomaru, where sweetness, smoke, spice, and a jerky-like finish overlap.

Tarot V THE HIEROPHANT

Distilled in 2021. Although still a heavy-peated release, the official profile shifts toward softness, coastal character, and harmony. The nose includes nori, rocky shoreline notes, straw hat, clean cask aroma, elegant smoke, pear, menthol, and a subtle prosciutto-and-cheese nuance. The palate is gentle, with nori, salted kelp, malty sweetness, lemon peel, black-tea candy, prosciutto, pear, and mint. Rather than being a power-driven peated whisky, it balances seaweed notes, malt sweetness, and mild smoke.

Tarot IV THE EMPEROR

Distilled in 2020 and made with Highland heavily peated malt. Compared with the Islay mineral character of Tarot III, Tarot IV places more emphasis on balance and structure. The nose shows ripe apple, pineapple, dense smoke, vanilla oak, hearth ash, pineapple candy, beeswax, apricot, and roasted chestnut. The palate is smooth and slightly oily, with ashy peat smoke supporting a weave of sweetness and smoke. This is a more stable and complete Saburomaru, combining delicate fruit with a firm smoky backbone.

Premium

Tarot III THE EMPRESS

Distilled in 2020. This is the first Tarot release to clearly express an Islay peat character. The nose shows new oak, vanilla, dry peat, peppercorn, Japanese pear, root vegetables, and pineapple. The palate brings a light saltiness, strong peat smoke, soft sweetness, and minerality. Complex yet clean, it combines smoke, maritime mineral character, and clear spirit quality, making it a key expression for understanding Saburomaru’s “Islay peat × ZEMON distillation” style.
