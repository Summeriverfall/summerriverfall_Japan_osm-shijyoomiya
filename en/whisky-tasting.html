Kyoto Shijo-Omiya

Vintage Japanese Whisky Tasting

A Japanese Whisky Journey Across Time and Place

Rare vintage bottles, guided comparisons, and a tasting experience built around the dimensions of time, place, and terroir. From historical inheritance, to the cold maritime character of Akkeshi and the mountain-and-sea fireworks of Saburomaru, onward to the future represented by Chichibu and the new generation of Japanese craft whisky.

In Kyoto Shijo-Omiya, discover a quieter and rarer side of Japanese whisky.

This is not simply a whisky flight. It is a journey through the past, present, and future of Japanese whisky. The past settles in the echoes of time, century-old lineage, layered expressions, and Taketsuru’s lifelong original spirit. The present lives in Akkeshi’s cold-sea seasons and blooms through Saburomaru’s mountain, sea, smoke, and fire. The future opens through Chichibu. And beyond them all, impermanence has its own order, turning like the Wheel of Fortune.

Each pour is arranged not only by rarity, but also by sequence, contrast, timing, and story. Through the glass, guests can trace the accumulation of Japan’s classic whisky houses, the contemporary expression of local terroir, and the unfinished growth of the new craft whisky era.

Why taste Japanese whisky here?

Because our collection traces the past, present, and future of Japanese whisky

Our Suntory and Nikka collections form a comprehensive system spanning multiple generations and product lines. We also preserve rare whiskies from closed Japanese distilleries, including precious original malts from different vintages and ages.

Alongside these historical bottles, we have selected representative bottlings from Japan’s new generation of craft distilleries as comparative tasting samples.

The depth of our Hibiki and Yamazaki collections alone far exceeds what is normally found in an ordinary bar. More importantly, many of these bottles are available to open and taste—not merely to be viewed.

Why taste Japanese whisky in Kyoto?

Because Hibiki and Yamazaki were born right here in Kansai

The Yamazaki Distillery is less than an hour away. Osaka has been Suntory's most important market since the brand's founding. Drinking Suntory here isn't just tasting whisky — it's experiencing a century of craft in the place where it all began.

Who leads the tasting experience?

More Than a Pour — A Conversation

With a background in investment banking and private equity, your host approaches whisky with a sharp eye for rarity, structure, and value. He has also worked across tea, dining, and hospitality, and brings together interests in marathon running, collecting, music, tea culture, antiques, vintage bottles, and Showa-era aesthetics. With a distinctly cross-cultural outlook and ease across multiple languages and dialects, he shapes each tasting with both depth and warmth.

Tasting Flights

A Journey Across Generations

Each flight is designed as a guided comparison across eras, styles, and distilleries—not simply as a collection of old or rare bottles. Historical references, modern expressions, and carefully selected contrast pours reveal how Japanese whisky has evolved.

An optional final pour is available for guests who wish to take the journey one step further.

Explore our tasting philosophy

For us, heritage means more than age or rarity. It means understanding where Japanese whisky came from, how it developed, how it flourished, how it declined, and how it was revived. It means tracing both the horizontal stories across distilleries and styles, and the vertical stories that unfold across time.

This is why our flights are built not only around bottles, but around origins, lineages, transitions, and continuity.

THEME 04

Origin & Legacy

First Resolve, Lifelong Devotion ～ Taketsuru

From Masataka Taketsuru's studies in Scotland to the creation of Yoichi and Miyagikyo, Taketsuru represents both the origin of Japanese whisky and the transmission of Nikka's founding spirit. Taketsuru Pure Malt combines Yoichi's weight and smoke, Miyagikyo's softness and fruit, and Nikka's blending philosophy. This is not simply an age-statement vertical. From the current white label and discontinued black label to the 12, 17 and 21 Year Old releases, the flight follows how the character of Nikka's two malt distilleries changes through maturation and formulation, while the Miyagikyo 10th Anniversary old bottle reveals the other source behind Taketsuru.

approx. 45–75 min

Current White-Label Taketsuru

Released on 31 March 2020 as the current reformulation. More modern and clearly defined than the black label, it combines fruit, substantial malt and gentle peat, finishing with slight dark-chocolate bitterness and wood.

Discontinued Black-Label Taketsuru

The NAS Taketsuru sold before the white-label reformulation and replaced in March 2020. It is more old-fashioned and rounded, integrating fruit, malty sweetness, vanilla-toned cask aromas and gentle smoke in a smooth palate.

Taketsuru 12 Year Old

Introduced in 2000 as the first age-statement Taketsuru and discontinued around 2014 in favour of the NAS release. Light and elegant, it emphasizes bright fruit, gentle malt sweetness, balance and drinkability.

Taketsuru 17 Year Old

Discontinued at the end of March 2020 and not restored as a regular product. Mature fruit, honey, vanilla, aged wood, light peat and gentle smoke combine in one of the clearest expressions of balance in the range.

Taketsuru 21 Year Old

Also discontinued at the end of March 2020 and not restored as a regular product. Deeper, softer and more complex, with ripe fruit, dried fruit, honey, wood, dark chocolate, light smoke and a long finish.

THEME 07

Regional Terroir

Fireworks Between Mountain and Sea ～ SAB.

Toyama, blue seas, evening skies and smoke: SAB. expresses Hokuriku's landscape and sensory colours through blending. Snow White, Sunset Red, Night Black, Dark Black and Ocean Blue unfold like fireworks between mountain and sea, moving from cool delicacy and sweetness to spice, darkness and maritime depth. Compared with the single malt and collectable orientation of the Tarot Series, SAB. is a more accessible entry into Saburomaru and is particularly well suited to highballs. This flight shows how the distillery converts its core smoky style into approachable yet distinctive blended whisky.

THEME 08

Bronze Distillation

The Order Beneath Impermanence ～ Saburomaru Tarot

From the small stills of a traditional sake brewery to ZEMON, the copper-tin alloy cast still that began full operation in 2019, the Saburomaru Tarot Series records more than bottle numbers and the sequence of the Major Arcana. It traces the distillery's passage through a major technological transition. Each release reflects a particular distillation vintage and stage of experimentation: standard-strength editions emphasize balance and maturation, while cask-strength editions retain more direct spirit power. III THE EMPRESS won Gold at TWSC 2024; IV THE EMPEROR and V THE HIEROPHANT won Gold at TWSC 2025; and IV THE EMPEROR Cask Strength received the top award in the Japanese single malt small-batch category at WWA 2025.

approx. 45–75 min

Regular

VIII THE STRENGTH

Made with Islay 80 ppm super-heavily peated malt, this is the Tarot release with the strongest maritime character and smoke. The 48% edition is relatively smooth, with vanilla, Japanese bekko candy and grapefruit pith.

VII THE CHARIOT

Made with Highland 80 ppm super-heavily peated malt, this is one of Saburomaru's most intensely peated releases. Burning wood, ash, fern, orange peel, ripe red apple and ginger lead into dense ashy peat and a powerful smoke-laden finish.

VI THE LOVERS

Using spirit distilled in 2020 and 2021, it combines Highland peat with Islay peat. Sweetness, smoke, spice and jerky-like savouriness overlap in a richer, more complex Saburomaru.

V THE HIEROPHANT

Distilled in 2021. Although still heavily peated, it is not driven by raw power; maritime savouriness, malt sweetness and restrained smoke remain in balance.

IV THE EMPEROR

Distilled in 2020 with Highland heavily peated malt. Compared with the Islay-derived minerality of III, IV places greater emphasis on balance and structure, combining delicate fruit with a firm smoky framework.

Premium

III THE EMPRESS

Distilled in 2020, this was the first Tarot release to clearly express an Islay-influenced peat character. Fresh oak, vanilla, dry peat, Japanese pear and pineapple meet slight salinity, powerful smoke, gentle sweetness and minerality. It is a key release for understanding “Islay peat × ZEMON distillation.”
